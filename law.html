            **Declaration of Independence of the State of Onion Union**

By the will of the people and the unbreakable bond of unity, we, the inhabitants of this fertile land, declare that we have broken the chains of oppression, rejecting all yokes that once bound us. In the dawn of a new age, we claim the soil beneath our feet, the fruits of our labor, and the sweat of our brows as our birthright. No force shall trample upon the dignity we hold sacred, for our hands are calloused from toil, our minds sharp with purpose, and our hearts burn with the fire of collective strength.

We are the harvest of our forebears' struggles, the wind that whispers freedom, and the earth that holds us firm. From the humble onion to the towering tree, every root and every leaf belongs to us all, equally, without exception. As we stand tall, we proclaim to the world that our anthem shall forever echo with the chords of *The Internationale*, a song of hope, solidarity, and victory. This land shall know no kings, no tyrants, and no masters—save the will of the people united.

In this spirit, we hereby establish the State of Onion Union, declaring ourselves free, sovereign, and indivisible, forever entwined by the bonds of equality and the righteous pursuit of justice. Let none seek to break this bond, for treachery shall be met with the swift and unyielding hand of justice.

So declared, under the watchful eyes of history, we pledge our lives, our labor, and our love to the State of Onion Union.

**Onion Union Legal Code**

**Article I: Supreme Unity and Authority**
1. The sovereignty of the State of Onion Union rests in its people, and all power shall be exercised in their name and for their benefit.
2. All resources, both natural and created by labor, shall be held in common and managed collectively for the prosperity and well-being of the people.
3. The governance of the State shall be guided by councils of workers and representatives, who shall be selected by the people to act on their behalf.
4. Decisions affecting the whole shall be made through the assembly of the people or by the councils, ensuring that no individual or group may act in a manner contrary to the will of the majority.

**Article II: Distribution of Resources and Labor**
1. Every citizen has the right and the duty to contribute their labor to the community according to their abilities, and in return, receive what they need to live a life of dignity.
2. No person shall accumulate excessive personal wealth or property beyond what is necessary for their daily life, for all surplus belongs to the people.
3. The fruits of all labor shall be shared according to the needs of the people, with priority given to the most vulnerable and those whose work supports the common good.

**Article III: Defense of the Union**
1. The defense of the State of Onion Union shall be the duty of all citizens. In times of danger, every able-bodied person is required to contribute to the defense and preservation of the Union.
2. Acts of treason, defined as any attempt to undermine the sovereignty, unity, or collective wellbeing of the Onion Union, shall be punished by death. This includes any action intended to overthrow the lawful authority of the people or sabotage the collective efforts of the State.
3. False accusations of treason or execution based on falsehoods shall themselves be considered acts of treason, subject to the same penalties.
4. The councils of workers shall be responsible for the investigation and trial of such offenses, ensuring fairness and justice while protecting the State from threats within.

**Article IV: Governance and Civic Participation**
1. The councils shall consist of representatives from all sectors of labor, elected by their peers to serve for a limited term. These representatives shall be accountable to the people and subject to recall if they fail to uphold the principles of the State.
2. All citizens have the right and duty to participate in decision-making processes that affect the community, whether through direct assembly or through the election of representatives.
3. Education, healthcare, housing, and all services essential to the well-being of the people shall be provided by the State as a shared responsibility, ensuring that no individual is left without the basic means to live.

**Article V: Culture and Symbolism**
1. The Anthem of the State of Onion Union shall be *The Internationale*, representing the ideals of unity, solidarity, and the global struggle for justice. It shall be sung at all official gatherings and public events.
2. The symbols of the State shall include the emblem of the onion, representing the layers of unity and strength that bind the people together.
3. The preservation and promotion of culture, art, and knowledge shall be a central tenet of the State, with all cultural contributions considered collective property and shared freely among the people.

**Article VI: Justice and Enforcement**
1. The justice system of the Onion Union shall be overseen by councils of workers and legal experts, with trials conducted fairly and transparently. The people shall have the final say in matters of guilt and innocence.
2. Crimes against the people, including theft of common resources, exploitation, and betrayal of the Union, shall be dealt with severely, with penalties ranging from reparations to death, depending on the gravity of the offense.
3. The penalty of death shall be reserved for the most serious crimes, such as treason, false execution, and actions that endanger the survival of the Union. Execution shall only be carried out after a thorough and public trial, with the right to appeal ensured for all accused.

**Article VII: Execution Procedure**

1. **Sentence Pronouncement:**
   - Following the final verdict in cases where the death penalty has been decreed for crimes such as treason, false execution, or actions gravely endangering the survival of the Union, the sentence of execution shall be publicly announced by the Council of Workers.
   - The condemned individual shall be informed of their rights, which include the right to make a final statement and the right to appeal within a defined period. If no appeal is lodged or if the appeal is denied, the execution will proceed.

2. **Firing Squad as Sole Method:**
   - The method of execution in the State of Onion Union shall be by **firing squad**, as a symbol of the collective action and will of the people.
   - The firing squad shall consist of the Chief Executor

3. **Preparation for Execution:**
   - The condemned shall be given the opportunity to address the Union one last time, in accordance with the principle of transparency. Their final words, if any, will be recorded and archived as part of the people's historical record.
   - The condemned will be bound at the hands and blindfolded, if they so choose, as a matter of dignity. They may also refuse the blindfold if they wish to face the firing squad directly.

4. **Location and Ceremony:**
   - Executions shall take place at dawn in a designated public space, where the people may gather to bear witness. The event will be overseen by a member of the Worker’s Justice Council, who shall declare the reasons for the execution and remind the people of the seriousness of the act.
   - The anthem, *The Internationale*, shall be played before the execution to honor the unity of the people and the laws that safeguard their freedom.

5. **Execution:**
   - Upon the signal from the designated officer, the firing squad will aim and fire at the heart.
   - Medical personnel will immediately confirm the death of the condemned. If the person survives the initial volley, a final shot to the ear will be administered by the officer in charge.

6. **Aftermath:**
   - The body of the executed shall be buried in an unmarked grave, in accordance with the law, with no commemoration. The act of treason or betrayal shall not be glorified, and no symbol shall be left to memorialize those who have sought to undermine the Union.
   - A public announcement shall be made, affirming the unity of the State and reminding all citizens of the importance of solidarity and loyalty to the Onion Union.

This procedure is established as a solemn reminder that the people of the Onion Union will protect their Union at all costs, maintaining justice with the utmost gravity and transparency.
